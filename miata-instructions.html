Miata Upholstery Installation

Step-by-step guide for installing your Miata seat kit

Important Notice

If you remove the old upholstery before your kit arrives, DO NOT throw anything away. Keep everything until your new kit is installed.

12-Step Installation Process

Follow these steps in order for best results. Click each step for detailed instructions.

1

Disassemble the Seat

Remove the seat from your vehicle and separate the backrest from the seat bottom. Take photos as you go to help with reassembly.

2

Paint the Seat Pans

While the seat is apart, it's a great time to clean and repaint the metal seat pans. This prevents rust and gives your installation a professional finish.

3

Remove the Old Upholstery

Carefully remove the old seat covers. Cut the hog rings with side cutters or pliers. Save the listing rods (metal wires) from the old covers - you'll reuse them.

4

Fixing the Foam

Critical step! Inspect and repair all foam damage before installing new covers.

Use the included cotton padding and spray glue to rebuild worn bolsters and cover any exposed metal. Failure to repair foam damage will void warranty.

5

Fixing Loose Wires

Check all the internal support wires in the seat. Reattach any loose wires and straighten any that are bent. These provide the internal structure for your seat.

6

Fixing Speakers (If Applicable)

If your Miata seats have headrest speakers, now is the time to repair or replace them. Fill empty speaker cavities with cotton padding if you don't want speakers.

7

Prepare the Headrests

Inspect the headrest foam and repair any damage. Fill speaker cavities with cotton padding to prevent the "caved in" look on the sides of the headrest.

8

Install the Backrest Upholstery

Slip the new backrest cover over the headrest first, then work it down over the backrest. Stuff extra cotton in the shoulders for a full appearance. Secure with hog rings.

9

Install the Bottom Upholstery

Position the new seat bottom cover and work it into place. Insert the listing rods into the channels and secure them with hog rings. Pull the cover tight for a smooth finish.

10

Re-Assemble the Seat

Bolt the backrest back to the seat bottom. Make sure you use the correct bolts in the correct locations - refer to photos you took during disassembly.

11

Finishing Touches

Reinstall the seat tracks, adjust the recline mechanism, and do a final inspection. Make sure all covers are smooth and properly secured.

12

How to Use Hog Rings

Hog rings secure the upholstery to the seat frame. Use the included hog ring pliers to crimp them closed. Place rings about 2-3 inches apart for secure attachment.
