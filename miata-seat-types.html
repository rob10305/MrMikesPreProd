Miata Seat Types

There are 2 types - know which you have before ordering

Important for Leather Kit Orders

When ordering leather kits, it is imperative that MrMike knows which type of seats you have. The two types require different patterns.

Can You Spot the Difference?

Pictured here are two original Miata seats (1990-1997).

Hint: The difference is not in the backrests (tops) - it's in the front of the seat bottoms.

Look at the front edge of each seat bottom carefully.

The Answer: Bullnose vs. Waterfall

Left seat has a rounded front. Right seat has a protruding front.

Red lines show the rounded front. Blue lines show the protruding front.

Bullnose Style

Rounded Front Edge

The front edge of the seat bottom is rounded and smooth, without a pronounced lip or overhang.

When ordering, specify: "Bullnose"

Waterfall Style

Protruding Front Edge

The front edge of the seat bottom has a pronounced lip that extends forward, creating a "waterfall" effect.

When ordering, specify: "Waterfall"

Side-by-side comparison with labels

How to Check Your Seats

  1. Look at the front edge of the seat bottom (where your thighs rest)
  2. If the front edge is smooth and rounded = Bullnose
  3. If the front edge has a pronounced lip/overhang = Waterfall
  4. Take a photo from the side if you're unsure - MrMike can help identify your seat type

MrMikes Kits Fit All 1990-1997 Miata Seats

Both Bullnose and Waterfall styles are supported - just let us know which type you have when ordering.
