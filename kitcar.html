Tips for Kit Car Interior Work

MrMike doesn't cover parts directly, but does sell bulk matching leathers and vinyls. Here are some pointers for your kit car project:

1. Install Parts First

Install everything (vents, gauges, brackets, door panels) into the car first. Do any trimming or alterations during this first install. When it's all perfect, THEN remove it and upholster the parts.

2. Check Clearances

Pay attention to clearances between dashboard-to-door panels, and door panels-to-door jamb trim. Contact points cause squeaks and upholstery eventually rubs through.

3. Use Closed Cell Foam

Cover fiberglass parts with a thin layer of Closed Cell Foam (Landau Pad) and sand smooth prior to upholstery. This gives the factory feel. Don't use sponge foam - it looks puffy and homemade.

4. Use High Heat Glue

Use high heat contact cement. Cars get very hot inside - cheap glue melts and all your work goes down the drain.
