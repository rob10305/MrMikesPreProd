Why Do-It-Yourself?

All pictures on MrMikes.com are of customer-installed seat kits. They did it, so can you - it's not hard!

If you "Do-it-yourself" you will "Do-it-right." If you take it to a professional, they often won't bother with the instructions and may turn out wrong.

The new upholstery arrives complete and ready to install - there is no sewing involved.

Seat Core Repair Instructions

Critical: Repair Core Damage

It is very important that any damage to the seat core be repaired (padded) to avoid premature wear of the new upholstery. Any metal bars and sharp edges must be padded. Failure to repair core damage will void your MrMikes warranty.

Instructions and supplies are included to repair damage to the seat core (the actual seat, after the old upholstery is removed). Pay particular attention to the driver's outboard bolster.

Core Repair Steps

  1. Expose the damage: Bolster damage is most common by the driver's door. Years of wear can cause the inner bar to eat at the foam from inside. Slit foam with a razor blade (included) to expose the metal bar.
  2. Pack with cotton: Pack cotton around the metal bar and fill any voids in the foam. Spray glue (included) into the cut, onto the cotton (included), and the foam. Pull foam up and around the bar to encase it.
  3. Cover with duct tape: Cover the cut with duct tape. Spray glue the foam first - this will make the tape stick well. The tape holds the cut closed.
  4. Add second layer: Spray the repaired area with more glue. Place a piece of cotton onto the wet glue, spray over the cotton, and cover with a second layer of duct tape.
  5. Test your work: When properly padded, it should be impossible to feel the metal bar when pressing your thumb on the area.
  6. Check entire seat: Press the foam in other areas of the seat, backrest, and cushion. If you feel metal, pad those areas too.
