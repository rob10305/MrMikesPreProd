Core Repair Instructions

Important: Repair Core Damage

Instructions and supplies are included to repair damage to the seat core (the actual seat, after the old upholstery is removed).

It is very important that any damage be repaired (padded) to avoid premature wear of the new upholstery. Any metal bars and sharp edges must be padded.

Pay particular attention to the driver's outboard bolster. Failure to repair core damage will void your MrMikes warranty.

8-Step Core Repair

1. Bolster damage is most common by the driver's door. Slit foam with razor blade to expose the metal bar.

2. Pack cotton around the metal bar and fill any voids. Spray glue and pull foam around the bar.

3. Cover the cut with duct tape. Spray glue the foam first - this makes the tape stick better.

4. You're not done yet! Spray the repaired area with more glue.

5. Place cotton onto wet glue. Spray glue over the cotton and down onto the foam.

6. Cover second layer with duct tape. Compress the cotton with tape as you go.

7. That's it! When properly padded, you should not feel the metal bar when pressing your thumb.

8. Press foam in other areas. If you feel metal, pad those areas until they look like #7.
