Tips & Tricks

Expert advice for getting the best results from your seat kit

1 Mounting Fiero Seats in Other Cars

When installing Fiero seats in a different vehicle, you'll need custom mounting brackets. Here are some tips:

  • Measure your existing seat mounting points carefully
  • Fiero seat tracks are 14" apart (center to center)
  • Fabricate adapter plates from 1/4" steel
  • Ensure the seat height allows proper headroom and visibility
  • Test fit before final welding or bolting

2 Always Repair the Foam First

This is the most important tip!

Before installing new upholstery, inspect and repair all foam damage. Look for:

  • Compressed or worn bolsters (especially driver's side)
  • Exposed metal bars or sharp edges
  • Torn or crumbling foam
  • Missing chunks of padding

Use the included cotton padding, spray glue, and duct tape to rebuild damaged areas. New upholstery over damaged foam will wear prematurely and void your warranty.

3 Swap the Seat Bottoms

The driver's seat bottom typically has more wear than the passenger side. You can swap them to even out the wear.

Important: Swapping does NOT magically repair damage! You still need to repair the worn driver's side bottom before putting it on the passenger side.

4 "The Bottom Has Fallen Out"

"The leather under my butt looks loose, do I need more padding?"

This is usually caused by broken or stretched suspension springs/straps inside the seat bottom, not a padding issue. Check and repair the seat's internal support structure before adding padding.

5 Bent, Broken, Missing, or Rusty Rods

The listing rods (metal rods that hold the upholstery in the seat grooves) may be damaged on old seats. Options:

  • Straighten bent rods carefully
  • Clean rusty rods with a wire brush and paint
  • Replace missing rods with similar diameter wire or rods from a donor seat

6 Pad the Leg Bolsters

The side bolsters on the seat bottom take a lot of abuse from getting in and out of the car. Even if they look okay, add extra padding to these areas to ensure longevity of your new covers.

7 Pad the Shoulders

The shoulder bolsters on the backrest also wear over time. Stuff extra cotton padding (included) into the shoulder areas when installing the new backrest cover for a fuller, like-new appearance.

8 Fixing Rips in Seat Foam

For tears and rips in the foam:

  1. Apply spray glue to both sides of the tear
  2. Press firmly together and hold
  3. Cover with duct tape for reinforcement
  4. For large missing sections, fill with cotton padding before taping

9 Glue the Side Back On

If a leg bolster flops over and has no support, the foam has likely separated from the seat frame. Use spray glue to reattach the foam to the frame, then reinforce with duct tape if needed.

10 Headrest Speaker Cavities

Why is the side of the headrest caved in?

The factory removed chunks of foam from '84 and '85 Fiero headrests for speaker cavities. Stuff cotton padding (included) inside the speaker cavity to fill it out before installing your new covers.

Don't want speakers? Fill the holes completely with cotton padding for a smooth appearance.

Want to add speakers? MrMikes can cut speaker holes in your new covers - just request this option when ordering.

11 Put the Bolts Back In (The Right Ones!)

When reassembling, make sure you use the correct bolts in the correct locations. Take photos during disassembly or organize bolts in labeled bags so you know exactly where each one goes.

12 Broken Pivot Pin Clips

The plastic clips that hold the pivot pins can become brittle and break. These can sometimes be found at auto parts stores or salvaged from donor seats. Handle them carefully during disassembly.

13 Paint the Badges

While you have the seats apart, it's a great time to refresh the recliner mechanism badges. Clean them up and touch up the paint to match your new interior color scheme.

14 Alter the Recliner Cover

The plastic recliner cover on Fiero seats can be modified or painted to better match your new upholstery. Some owners remove them entirely for a cleaner look, or paint them to match their interior color.
